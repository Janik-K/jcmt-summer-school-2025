Welcome!

We are pleased to host the first JCMT Summer School for the UK Community at University College London from the 8-9th of September 2025.

The aim of this summer school is to make the JCMT (James Clerk Maxwell Telescope) accessible to all UK astronomers with talks and workshops on its science capabilities, instrumentation, proposal application process, and data reduction.

The JCMT, the world's largest single-dish submillimetre telescope, operates a suite of instruments providing continuum, polarimetry and heterodyne capabilities, operating between 0.45 – 1.4mm (660 – 200 GHz). The JCMT is currently open to the entire UK community, and anyone affiliated with a UK university can propose for time on it.

This summer school is intended to be an introduction for new users and a refresher for those who may have used the JCMT before but in recent years have not had access to it. We especially encourage Early Career Researchers (ECRs) to attend.
